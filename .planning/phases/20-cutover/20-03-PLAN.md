<objective>
Remove every Docker-layer reference to the dead aniwatch and consumet containers. Per ROADMAP success criteria 2 + 3 and 20-CONTEXT.md decisions: the two service blocks (`aniwatch:` and `consumet:`) in `docker/docker-compose.yml`, the catalog's `ANIWATCH_API_URL` / `CONSUMET_API_URL` env entries, the catalog's `depends_on` entries pointing at those containers, the `patch-aniwatch.sh` shim script, and the corresponding lines in `docker/.env.example` must be GONE. The `megacloud-extractor` Dockerfile entrypoint reverts to the canonical `node server.js` (it already is — see step below).

The Makefile's `redeploy-%` recipe currently has a hardcoded HiAnime Redis cache purge wired to the catalog redeploy path (lines 277-281 of Makefile). That purge logic moves to a one-shot script in plan 20-05; this plan REMOVES it from the Makefile because the dead namespace no longer exists.

Purpose: Free the ~250MB combined image weight from the two dead containers, eliminate two failing health checks that currently confuse `make health`, and clean up the `make redeploy-catalog` muscle memory.

Output: A `docker compose up -d` brings up the full stack WITHOUT the two dead containers, the catalog service starts without ANIWATCH_API_URL / CONSUMET_API_URL, and `docker compose ps` confirms two fewer containers running.
</objective>
