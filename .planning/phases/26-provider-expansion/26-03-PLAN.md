<objective>
Produce a research artifact at `.planning/research/2026-05-19-en-source-survival.md` that evaluates every 2026 EN-source candidate worth considering for the scraper failover pool — at minimum: Miruro, AnimeOwl, AnimeFever, AniWatchTV (post-March 2026 USTR shutdown — confirm or deny), HiAnime (confirm dead per v3.0 Phase 20 removal, or document resurrection), Crunchyroll-Free (legal status verdict only). Each candidate gets a `live | dead | uncertain` status, a `worth-implementing | not-worth | needs-deeper-PoC` recommendation, anti-bot posture summary, and (if recommended) the embed extractors it needs + an implementation-effort estimate.

The sweep ends with an explicit **Decision Gate** prompting the operator to pick 0-2 survivors. The picks gate whether 26-04 (survey candidate #1) and 26-05 (survey candidate #2) execute — per CONTEXT.md D2, we do NOT pre-commit to "implement all live candidates." If the sweep yields zero `worth-implementing` candidates, SCRAPER-HEAL-26 ships as research-only and the phase moves on with two new providers (AllAnime from 26-01, plus AnimeKai if 26-06 converges).

Purpose: SCRAPER-HEAL-26 — fresh 2026 EN-source survival sweep with an operator decision gate. Without this gate, we either over-commit (implement all candidates, half of which turn out broken under load) or under-commit (skip the survey entirely and ship Phase 26 with just AllAnime). The gate keeps the engineering budget bounded.

Output: A markdown research artifact under `.planning/research/` (NOT under the phase directory, per CONTEXT.md "Output structure"), committed to origin/main, with a verdict matrix + per-survivor PoC sketches + a final Decision Gate section. Plan 26-03 is `autonomous: false` because the Decision Gate requires explicit operator input before Wave 3a/3b can execute.
</objective>
